Advancing Materials Science through Molecular Dynamics Simulations

As a Prime Minister Research Fellow at IIT Kharagpur, my work is dedicated to exploring the fundamental properties of materials at the nanoscale. My research leverages advanced Molecular Dynamics Simulations and Computational Materials Science to understand phenomena in nanofluidics, thermal transport, and the development of sustainable energy materials.

Featured Publications & Research

Significantly Reduced Thermal Conductivity and Enhanced Thermoelectric Performance of Twisted Bilayer Graphene

Published: July 2023

This research explores novel approaches to manipulate thermal transport in 2D materials, demonstrating how twisting graphene layers can lead to significant reductions in thermal conductivity. This work opens new avenues for designing high-efficiency thermoelectric devices and advanced thermal management solutions.

View Publication Explore Project

Mapping Fluid Structuration to Flow Enhancement in Nanofluidic Channels

Published: May 2023

Our study delves into the fundamental mechanisms governing fluid behavior at the nanoscale, revealing how molecular ordering within nanofluidic channels can dramatically enhance flow rates. These findings have significant implications for advanced filtration, energy harvesting, and microfluidic device design.
