Featured Research

My research focuses on the **Modulation of Interfacial Properties in 2D Materials**. This area opens vast avenues of applications, directly contributing to the United Nations' goals of water and energy availability. My aim is to achieve water and energy security through the development of sustainable technologies and by improving the accessibility and efficiencies of currently available technologies. To achieve these goals, I am working at the bottom-up approach of modifying properties at the nanoscale.

1. Supercapacitor for Sustainable Environment

This research highlights the use of Molecular Dynamics (MD) to investigate the local structure of electrolytes, specifically focusing on the interactions between solutes and the arrangement of ions within the electrolyte solutions. This analysis is essential for determining how variations in NaClO4 and ethylene glycol (EG) concentrations affect the electrochemical performance of the supercapacitors.

Status: Published (doi: 10.1002/smll.202400975)

2. Functionalised Graphene membrane Desalination: Molecular Dynamics Perspective

In this study, we take a graphene nanopore with varying functionalization to study ion-water interaction near the pore. The study is aimed towards pressure-based water desalination. The simulation setup involves a fixed graphene membrane with pressure force applied on the ion-water mixture. The Van der Waals interactions along with energy barrier emerge to be of prime importance.

Status: Published (doi: 10.1039/D2NR05552J)

3. Two-dimensional materials interactions and combining different

Twisted Bilayer Graphene

We take two layers of graphene and rotate one of them to study variations in various properties, investigating the impact of twist angle on their fundamental characteristics.

Status: Published

Graphene MoSeS Sandwiched Layer

We arrange two layers, one graphene and one MoSeS, and study their combined behavior and emergent properties in a sandwiched configuration.

Status: On-going

4. Understanding the Mechanics of complex topology of the 3D printed Anthill architecture

This work studies the deformation behavior of complex anthill geometries, which are generated using a cement casting method. Stress and deformation of these geometries are studied using Ansys Workbench. My contribution to this work includes validation and performing stress analysis studies in CFD software.

Status: Published (doi: 10.1093/oxfmat/itac003)
